Deadlines in online courses arrive fast — often faster than students expect. Unlike a lecture that reminds you something is due, Canvas just closes the submission window. Check due dates every week, not the day before.

Warning signs to watch for in yourself

Skipping announcements or not logging in for several days
Waiting until the day of the deadline to start reading or writing
Missing one assignment, then disengaging from the course entirely
Going silent — not emailing your instructor or participating in discussions
Letting readings pile up with a plan to "catch up on the weekend"
Feeling like you're too far behind to bother — you're not. Contact someone.

Strategies that actually work

1
Schedule study time like a class. Block it in your calendar with a specific start and end time. "I'll study when I have time" means you won't study.
2
Break assignments into smaller tasks. "Write essay" is not a task — it's a project. "Write thesis sentence," "outline three body points," and "draft introduction" are tasks you can actually do in a single session.
3
Check Canvas every day. Even 5 minutes — scan announcements, check for new feedback, confirm nothing is due tomorrow you forgot about.
4
Protect at least two solid study blocks per course per week. Shorter daily sessions beat a single four-hour Sunday marathon — and leave you less desperate when life intervenes.
5
Act immediately when you fall behind. One missed assignment is recoverable. Three missed assignments and two weeks of silence is not. The moment you slip, reach out.
