Not sure who to contact? Work through these scenarios

"Your internet goes out 30 minutes before an assignment is due. What do you do?"
Technical + Instructor
First, activate your Plan B from Module 2 — go to your backup internet location immediately. If you make it and submit late, email your instructor right away explaining what happened and when you submitted. Most instructors are understanding when you communicate proactively. If you can't get internet at all, email your instructor from your phone before the deadline — don't go silent.
"You've missed two assignments because of a work schedule change. What now?"
Instructor + Advising
Contact your instructor today — not after the next assignment. Explain the situation honestly and ask if any late work is accepted. Then contact your academic advisor to understand how the missed work affects your grade and what your options are. Don't disappear after a hard week — that's when students fall into a cycle that ends in withdrawal.
"You don't understand the feedback on a graded assignment. Who do you contact?"
Your Instructor
Email your instructor directly. Reference the specific assignment and the comment you don't understand — copy and paste it into your email if it helps. Ask something concrete: "You mentioned my thesis was unclear — can you help me understand what a stronger thesis would look like here?" Instructors appreciate specific questions far more than "I don't understand my grade."
"You're feeling overwhelmed and considering dropping out. Who can help?"
Student Care + Counseling + Advising
Please reach out before making any decisions. Contact Student Care — they exist for exactly this moment and can connect you to emergency support, counseling, and academic options you may not know about. Withdrawing has financial aid and academic consequences that are hard to undo. Talking to someone first costs nothing and might change everything.
