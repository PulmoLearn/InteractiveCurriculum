Technology & LMS Survival Skills

20–25 min
This module won't cover everything about Canvas — that would take hours. Instead, we'll focus on the five things you need to find on day one, plus how to build a solid backup plan for when technology fails.

Canvas: the five things you need to find first

1
Open your course in Canvas
From the Dashboard, click on any course tile. Your enrolled courses appear here after your institution activates them.
2
Look for "Announcements" in the left sidebar
Every course has its own announcements section. Instructors post updates, schedule changes, and reminders here — not via email.
Check this every time you log in
3
Turn on notification alerts
Go to Account → Notifications and enable announcements via email or text. Don't rely on memory — let Canvas notify you.
1
Click "Assignments" in the course sidebar
This lists every graded task in the course — discussions, quizzes, papers, and projects — grouped by type or week.
2
Read the full instructions before starting
Instructors put critical details — file format, word count, rubric — inside the assignment page, not in separate documents.
Most submission errors come from skipping this step
3
Submit early — don't wait until the deadline
Canvas closes submissions at the exact due time. A slow upload or browser issue 5 minutes before midnight is not an acceptable excuse most instructors will accept.
1
Find "Grades" in the course sidebar
Canvas shows your running grade and a breakdown of every scored item. Not all instructors post grades immediately — check the syllabus for their timeline.
2
Understand "What-If" scores
Canvas lets you enter hypothetical scores to see how future assignments would affect your grade. Use it to plan ahead, not to rationalize falling behind.
Available under the Grades view
1
Open the graded assignment
Click on a returned assignment in Grades. Your score and any rubric scoring will appear on the right side of the page.
2
Look for inline comments
Instructors often leave comments directly on your document. Look for highlighted sections or margin notes — these are the most specific feedback you'll get.
Don't just look at the score — read the comments
1
Use the Canvas Calendar
Click the calendar icon in the global sidebar. Every assignment due date from every course shows up here — color-coded by course.
2
Export to your personal calendar
Canvas can sync to Google Calendar, Outlook, or Apple Calendar via an iCal feed. Go to Calendar → Calendar Feed to get your personal link.
Set this up in week 1 — not week 7
3
Check the full Canvas Student Guide
For anything else in Canvas, the official student guide covers every feature with step-by-step screenshots.

Technology requirements

Browser
Chrome or Firefox (current version). Safari works for most things. Avoid Internet Explorer entirely.
Internet
Minimum 5 Mbps for video. 25+ Mbps recommended. Test yours at fast.com.
Webcam & mic
Required for some courses (proctored exams, video discussions). Check your syllabus.
File types
Submit .docx or .pdf unless your instructor says otherwise. Google Docs links are often not accepted.
Mobile limitations
The Canvas app is useful for reading — not for submitting assignments or taking quizzes. Use a laptop or desktop for coursework.
